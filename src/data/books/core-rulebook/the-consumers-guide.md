# Money

The Empire mints coins in three denominations: Brass Pennies (d), Silver Shillings (/–), and Gold Crowns (GC).

| Unit | Value |
| --- | --- |
| 1 Gold Crown (1GC) | 20 Silver Shillings |
| 1 Silver Shilling (1/–) | 12 Brass Pennies |
| 1 Gold Crown | 240 Brass Pennies |

## Availability

Every Trapping has an Availability: Common, Scarce, Rare, or Exotic. Common items are always in stock. Scarce/Rare items require an Availability Test to find locally; Exotic items are only available via GM fiat, Commission, or Crafting.

| Availability | Village | Town | City |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Common | In Stock | In Stock | In Stock |
| Scarce | 30% | 60% | 90% |
| Rare | 15% | 30% | 45% |
| Exotic | Not in Stock | Not in Stock | Not in Stock |

Rule of thumb for stock quantity: villages have 1 item, towns have 1d10, cities have as many as the GM deems appropriate (doubled for Common, halved, rounding up, for Rare).

## Buying and Selling

Evaluate identifies item quality, appraises coins, and estimates Rare/Exotic prices to within +/-10%. Haggle is an Opposed Test; winning reduces price by 10% (up to 20% on an Astounding Success or with Dealmaker).

Selling uses the same Availability procedure, but for a buyer. Base sale price is half the listed price. Each time the seller halves the asking price further, the buyer's Availability improves by one step.

### Bartering Ratios

| Traded Items | vs. Common | vs. Scarce | vs. Rare | vs. Exotic |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Common | 1:1 | 2:1 | 4:1 | 8:1 |
| Scarce | 1:2 | 1:1 | 2:1 | 4:1 |
| Rare | 1:4 | 1:2 | 1:1 | 2:1 |
| Exotic | 1:8 | 1:4 | 1:2 | 1:1 |

## Craftsmanship

A Trapping with more Qualities than Flaws is a Quality Trapping; a Trapping with more Flaws than Qualities is a Flawed Trapping.

- Each Item Quality: double the price, one step less available.
- Each Item Flaw: halve the price, one step more available (Exotic items keep their Availability regardless of Flaws).

### Item Qualities

| Quality | Effect |
| --- | --- |
| Durable | Item takes +1 (or more, stacking) Damage before suffering penalties, and gains a breakage saving throw (9+ per instance, improving by 1 each additional time taken) |
| Fine | Marks superior craftsmanship/status; can be taken multiple times for greater effect |
| Lightweight | –1 Encumbrance |
| Practical | Failed Test with the item gains +1 SL; armour penalties reduced by one level |

### Item Flaws

| Flaw | Effect |
| --- | --- |
| Ugly | Related Fellowship Tests may suffer –10 |
| Shoddy | Item breaks on any failed Test rolling a double; Shoddy armour breaks on any Critical Hit to a location it protects |
| Unreliable | Failed Test using the item gets –1 SL; wearing Unreliable armour doubles its penalties |
| Bulky | +1 Encumbrance (worn clothing/armour count as Enc 1 even when worn); armour Fatigue penalties doubled |

## Encumbrance

Items are rated 0–3 Encumbrance points. Carrying capacity without penalty equals Strength Bonus + Toughness Bonus (average humans: about 6).

| Enc | Penalty |
| --- | --- |
| Up to limit | No penalties |
| Up to double limit | –1 Movement (min 3), –10 Agility, +1 Travel Fatigue |
| Up to triple limit | –2 Movement (min 2), –20 Agility (min 10), +2 Travel Fatigue |
| More than 3x limit | Not moving |

Worn items (armour, clothing, jewellery) drop Enc by 1 (often to 0). Money weighs 1 Enc per 200 coins. Oversized items (barrels, saddlebags) weigh 4+ Enc and usually only one may be carried.

## Weapon Qualities

| Quality | Effect |
| --- | --- |
| Accurate | +10 to any Test firing this weapon |
| Blackpowder | Target must pass an Average (+20) Cool Test or gain Broken, even on a miss |
| Blast (Rating) | All within (Rating) yards of the struck point take SL+Damage and any inflicted Conditions |
| Damaging | Damage may use the higher of the units die or the SL |
| Defensive | +1 SL to any Melee Test opposing an incoming attack |
| Distract | Successful hit pushes the target back 1 yard per SL won, instead of causing Damage |
| Entangle | Successful hit gives the target Entangled (Strength value = attacker's Strength); cannot also damage while entangling |
| Fast | May attack outside normal Initiative order; opposing Melee Tests against a non-Fast weapon suffer –10; never also Slow |
| Hack | On a hit, damages a struck piece of armour/shield by 1 point in addition to wounding |
| Impact | Adds the attack roll's units die to Damage; never also Undamaging |
| Impale | Causes a Critical Hit on any multiple of 10, or on doubles rolled at or under the Test target |
| Penetrating | Non-metal APs ignored; first point of all other armour ignored |
| Pistol | Can be used to attack in Close Combat |
| Precise | +1 SL on a successful attack Test with this weapon |
| Pummel | Head hit allows an Opposed Strength/Endurance Test; winning inflicts Stunned |
| Repeater (Rating) | Holds (Rating) shots, auto-reloading until exhausted, then requires a full reload |
| Shield (Rating) | Counts as (Rating) Armour Points on all locations while opposing an attack; Shield 2+ can also oppose missile shots in Line of Sight |
| Trap Blade | A Critical scored while defending can trap the attacker's blade instead of causing a Critical Hit (Opposed Strength Test + previous SL to disarm; Astounding Success breaks the blade unless Unbreakable) |
| Unbreakable | Will not break, corrode, or dull under almost any circumstance |
| Wrap | Opposing Melee Tests suffer –1 SL |

## Weapon Flaws

| Flaw | Effect |
| --- | --- |
| Dangerous | Any failed Test rolling a 9 on either die causes a Fumble |
| Imprecise | –1 SL when attacking; never also Precise |
| Reload (Rating) | Unloaded weapon needs an Extended Ranged Test reaching (Rating) SL to reload; interruption restarts reloading |
| Slow | Always strikes last in a Round; opponents get +1 SL to defend |
| Tiring | Impact/Damaging benefits only apply on a Charge Turn |
| Undamaging | APs are doubled against this weapon; no automatic minimum 1 Wound on a hit; never also Damaging or Impact |

Impaling ranged ammunition lodges in the target: arrows/bolts need a Challenging (+0) Heal Test to remove, bullets need a surgeon; unremoved ammunition blocks healing 1 Wound per shot.

Melee weapons are further grouped by skill (Melee (Flail), Melee (Polearm), etc.); using an unskilled Group means testing Weapon Skill and losing access to the weapon's Qualities (but not its Flaws).

- **Cavalry**: two-handed Cavalry weapons count as Two-Handed when used unmounted; one-handed Cavalry weapons aren't normally used unmounted.
- **Flail**: unskilled use adds the Dangerous Flaw and drops all listed Qualities.
- **Parry**: any one-handed Defensive weapon can be used with Melee (Parry) to oppose an attack without the usual off-hand penalty.
- **Engineering**: usable with Ranged (Blackpowder), but lose all Qualities while keeping Flaws.
- Ranged (Crossbow) or Ranged (Throwing) can be attempted at Ballistic Skill even without the specialism, losing Qualities but keeping Flaws. Ranged (Engineering) can use Blackpowder and Explosive weapons without penalty.

# Hirelings

Hirelings are NPCs hired for coin; any Career can be hired with GM permission. Pay equals their Social Status for a quick job, or triple that for a full day. Dangerous jobs demand double pay unless a Leadership Test succeeds (also required for complex independent tasks or good reason to flee). Quick Hireling profiles: add 5 Advances to every Career Characteristic/Skill per Career level hired, and 1 Talent per level.

## Henchmen

Henchmen are permanent party companions (hirelings, friends, or pets) with full character sheets, tied to one player. A Henchman earns half its attached player's XP (rounded down), spent by that player, and can serve as a replacement character if the original PC dies.
